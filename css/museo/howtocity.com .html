A good how-to guide isn't just a list of instructions. Here are a few things to keep in mind as you go about making your very first guide.

Think about the objectives of your guide. Setting intentions for the guide you want to create will help you make decisions about content along the way. Think about whether you may actually need more than one guide to explain a complex issue. Do you want to demystify a process and make it more transparent and easy to understand? Answer Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) that your organization receives around a specific topic? Share information about a new initiative or program and encourage participation?

Get the right people involved. Your guide might need input from a variety of people, from community members to city officials. Make sure you reach out to people who can help make sure that information in the guide is accurate and easy to use. You should definitely have a test audience review the guide before it is finalized.

Prepare the content of your guide You can always log back in another time to access your guide in process, but it also helps to start with a good amount of information. Click here for a list of the content that you will need to provide to complete the guide.
